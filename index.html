About Me

Hi! My name is Connor and I'm a sophomore at Dartmouth College majoring in Computer Science and Engineering. I am from Washington State where I graduated among the top in my high school class at Bellevue High School. During high school, I fell in love with chemisty and performed research over my summers at the Virginia Merrill Bloedel Hearing Center, and Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center.

In my junior year of high school, I took AP Computer Science and became fascinated with computer programming and theory. Combined with my interest in entrepreneurship, I see computer science as the vehicle that will allow me to most effectively build the tools I want and solve problems that will impact lives.

I have developed my computer science skills through an advanced course in my senior year of high school, Dartmouth coursework, and interning as a software developer at Mailprotector in 2018. Looking forward, I plan on building independent work and getting involved in research at Dartmouth to further sharpen my CS skills.

During my freshman year at Dartmouth, I participated in the Dartmouth Entrepreneurship Network Living Learning Community, where my classmates and I learned about entrepreneurship from successful Dartmouth alumni as well as current students working on ventures.

For fun, I study the tenor sax in the Coast Jazz Ensemble and independent study with Fred Haas, play lacrosse with the Dartmouth Club Lacrosse team, and I enjoy watersports as well as snowsports.
